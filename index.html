Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Top Level Analysis

  • Poverty: There appears to be a slight positive correlation between poverty and all three of the factors looked at. States with a higher % of their population in poverty, also experience higher levels of obesity, smoking and lack of healthcare.
  • Age: There appears to be a positive correlation between age and smoking. The older the population, the more likely they are to smoke. There is a negative correlation between age and healthcare. The older a population, the more likely they are to have healthcare. There does not appear to be a correlation between age and obesity.
  • Household Income: There appears to be a negative correlation between household income and obesity. The same can be said for smoking. The poorer the population, the more likely it is to be obese and smoke.

Nicole Cook, 2020
